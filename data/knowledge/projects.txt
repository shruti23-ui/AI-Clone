4. Medical MRI Classification — Deep Learning Research
   Institution: Digital Image & Sensing Lab, Technical University of Crete, Greece
   Tech Stack: Python, PyTorch, TensorFlow, OpenCV
   - Classified Focal Cortical Dysplasia Type II (FCD-II) from brain MRI scans using deep learning
   - Engineered channel attention mechanisms with VGG16, VGG19, and DenseNet201
   - Achieved 96.8% precision and 94.5% recall on 15,000+ MRI datasets
   - Research paper co-authored, under review at IEEE JBHI (IEEE Journal of Biomedical and Health Informatics)
   - This was part of the Erasmus+ Visiting Student program (5% acceptance rate)
   What it does: A deep learning model that automatically classifies brain MRI scans to detect Focal Cortical Dysplasia Type II — a neurological condition that causes epilepsy and is hard to diagnose visually. The model uses channel attention with VGG16, VGG19, and DenseNet201 architectures to highlight diagnostically relevant regions.
   Why it matters: FCD-II is notoriously difficult to detect on MRI scans even for expert radiologists. Automating detection at 96.8% precision can directly improve patient outcomes and reduce diagnostic delay.
   Research impact: Paper submitted to IEEE JBHI, one of the top journals in biomedical imaging and AI.
   Key innovation: Channel attention mechanism that focuses the network on subtle MRI signal differences characteristic of FCD-II lesions.

6. Shruti AI Clone (This project — AI Portfolio)
   Tech Stack: Next.js 14, TypeScript, LangChain, Google Gemini, Pinecone (vector store), TalkingHead 3D avatar, Google TTS, Groq Whisper (voice input)
   - Built an interactive AI portfolio where a 3D avatar speaks and answers questions about Shruti's work, research, and experience
   - Implemented RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) to ground responses in accurate knowledge about projects, resume, and research
   - Integrated voice input (Groq Whisper) and voice output (Google TTS) for fully conversational interaction
   - 3D talking avatar with real-time lip sync powered by TalkingHead.mjs and a custom GLB avatar model
   - Deployed on Vercel at ai-clone-beta.vercel.app
   What it does: Instead of a traditional portfolio website, Shruti built an AI clone of herself — a 3D avatar that speaks and answers any question about her background, projects, research, and how to contact her. Users can type or speak their questions and the avatar responds in voice.
   Why it matters: Demonstrates advanced full-stack AI engineering — combining LLMs, RAG, vector search, TTS, STT, 3D rendering, and real-time streaming in a single production application.
   Key technical features:
   - RAG pipeline: LangChain + Google Gemini embeddings + vector store for grounded, accurate answers
   - Streaming responses: Server-sent events for real-time text streaming
   - Voice I/O: Groq Whisper for transcription, Google TTS for audio generation
   - 3D avatar: Custom GLB model with TalkingHead lip sync and jaw animation
   - Pre-fetched TTS: Audio starts fetching before response finishes streaming to reduce perceived latency
